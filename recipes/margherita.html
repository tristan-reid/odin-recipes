Italian Margherita Pizza

Go Back

Description

An Italian Margherita pizza is the most iconic style of Neapolitan pizza, known for its simplicity, freshness, and balance of flavors. It’s made with a thin, soft, and slightly chewy crust that puffs up around the edges when baked at very high heat.

It’s celebrated as the perfect example of how a few high-quality ingredients can create something extraordinary. The following section contains the ingredients list for this dish.

Ingredients

For Dough:

  • 2 cups (250 g) all-purpose or “00” flour
  • 3/4 cup (180 ml) warm water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast (or 8 g fresh yeast)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For Topping:

  • 1/2 cup (120 g) canned San Marzano tomatoes (crushed or pureed)
  • 6–8 oz (170–225 g) fresh mozzarella (preferably mozzarella di bufala)
  • 6–8 fresh basil leaves
  • 1–2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

Step 1: Make the Dough

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for about 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt.
  3. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil, then mix until a sticky dough forms.
  4. Knead on a floured surface for about 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise for 1–2 hours, or until doubled in size.

Step 2: Prepare The Sauce

  1. Crush or blend San Marzano tomatoes into a smooth puree.
  2. Add a pinch of salt (and optionally a drizzle of olive oil).

Step 3: Prepare the Toppings

  1. Slice the fresh mozzarella into thin pieces.
  2. Pat lightly with a paper towel to remove excess moisture (prevents soggy pizza).
  3. Keep fresh basil leaves whole.

Step 4: Shape the Pizza

  1. Preheat your oven as hot as it can go (ideally 475–500°F / 250–260°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it inside while preheating.
  2. Punch down the risen dough and divide into balls (1 ball = 1 pizza).
  3. On a floured surface, stretch or roll one ball into a thin circle (about 12 inches).

Step 5: Assemble

  1. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving about 1 inch around the edge.
  2. Evenly distribute the mozzarella slices.
  3. Add a few fresh basil leaves.
  4. Drizzle lightly with olive oil.

Step 6: Bake

  1. Transfer the pizza to the hot oven (onto the stone or a baking sheet).
  2. Bake for 7–10 minutes (or 90 seconds if using a wood-fired oven)
  3. Remove from oven and let cool for 1–2 minutes before slicing.

Step 7: Serve

  1. Garnish with a touch more fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil.
  2. Serve immediately — Italian pizza is best eaten hot and fresh!
